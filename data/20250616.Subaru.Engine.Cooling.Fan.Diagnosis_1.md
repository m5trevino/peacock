## Subaru Engine Cooling Fan Troubleshooting - The Real Guide

**System Overview:** Subaru cooling fans are ECU-controlled based on coolant temp, AC operation, and other factors. Most got 2-speed fans or dual fans depending on model/year.

### The Foundation - How This Shit Actually Works

The ECU monitors coolant temperature through the temperature sensor. When coolant hits around 190-200Â°F, ECU grounds the fan relay control circuit, energizing the relay and sending 12V to the fan motor. Simple as that - but the devil's in the details, my boy.

### Primary Failure Points (In Order of Frequency)

1. **Fan Motor Itself** - Bearings wear out, windings fail
2. **Temperature Sensor Drift** - Gives wrong readings to ECU  
3. **Relay Failure** - Contacts burn out, coil fails
4. **Wiring/Connector Issues** - Corrosion, broken wires
5. **ECU Problems** - Rare but happens

### Diagnostic Flow - The Systematic Approach

**Step 1: Verify the Real Problem**
Don't assume fan failure just 'cause it ain't running. Check if engine actually overheats or if you just noticed fan not spinning. Verify coolant level, thermostat operation first.

**Step 2: Direct Fan Motor Test**
- Disconnect fan electrical connector
- Apply 12V directly to fan motor terminals
- Good motor spins smooth, draws normal amps (usually 8-15A)
- Bad motor = no spin, excessive current, or grinding noise

**Step 3: Temperature Sensor Testing**
This is where most fuckers mess up - gotta test resistance at different temps:
- 68Â°F (20Â°C): 2300-2700 ohms
- 176Â°F (80Â°C): 300-400 ohms  
- 212Â°F (100Â°C): 180-220 ohms

Use a thermometer in the coolant and ohmmeter on sensor terminals. Resistance should drop smoothly as temp rises.

**Step 4: Relay Testing**
Standard 4-pin automotive relay:
- Pins 85/86: Control (ECU grounds this when fan needed)
- Pins 30/87: Power (battery voltage to fan)
- Apply 12V to 85/86, should click and show continuity 30/87

**Step 5: ECU Control Testing**
Check if ECU is actually commanding fan operation:
- Should ground relay control when coolant reaches threshold
- Scan tool shows commanded vs actual fan status
- Look for cooling system DTCs

### Model-Specific Intelligence

**2004 Forester EJ25:** Single fan system, ECU-controlled via temp input. Common failures are fan motor bearings and temp sensor drift.

**Legacy/Outback (2000-2009):** Dual fan setup (main + AC condenser). More complex control logic - test each fan independently.

### Pro-Level Troubleshooting Tips

- **Thermostat First:** Always verify thermostat operation before assuming fan problem
- **Proper Bleeding:** Air pockets after service cause overheating that can damage fans
- **ECU Relearn:** May need to cycle after component replacement
- **Coolant Leaks:** Minor leaks cause overheating that kills fans over time

### Common DTCs You'll See
